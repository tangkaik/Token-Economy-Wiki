## 全文

Most crypto projects live and die by speculation. io.net is proving there's another way.

io.net has officially surpassed $20M in annualized verifiable, on-chain revenue. Not token trades. Not TVL sleight of hand. Not OTC or market maker deals. **Real revenue from real customers paying for decentralized compute they actually use.**

This makes io.net something of a unicorn in its niche. The crypto industry has spent years chasing narratives while struggling to demonstrate genuine product-market fit. DePIN was supposed to change that by anchoring digital networks to physical infrastructure and real-world utility. But talk is cheap. Revenue is proof—especially as Microsoft's CTO calls the current AI compute shortage "a massive crunch".

io.net's $20M milestone shows that decentralized infrastructure can compete directly with centralized incumbents and win. With over 139,000 GPUs distributed across more than 138 countries, io.net is now generating revenue by serving AI developers, startups, and enterprises that need compute without the markup, delays, or lock-in that come with traditional cloud providers like AWS or GCP.

**The revenue validates three critical assumptions about the future of infrastructure:**

First, Web2 developers will adopt Web3 technologies when the value proposition is undeniable. io.net's customer base includes AI startups, ML engineers, and technical teams that care about cost, speed, and flexibility. io.net delivers all three, aligning with the RWA and DePIN sectors that now exceed $50 billion in market cap as crypto infrastructure proves its real-world utility.

Second, decentralization isn't just a philosophical talking point. It's a structural advantage. By aggregating underutilized compute across a global network, io.net offers enterprise-grade GPUs at up to 70% less cost than AWS or GCP. That's not a subsidy model. That's structural economics, and it's eroding Big Tech's compute monopoly one workload at a time.

Third, sustainable Web3 businesses are possible. Revenue-driven growth proves that crypto infrastructure can scale without relying on token incentives alone. io.net's model works because customers pay for value delivered, not promises made. This makes io.net one of the few crypto projects generating revenue from product adoption rather than token speculation. Can you say 'bullish'?

The implications extend beyond io.net. $20M in revenue proves market demand for decentralized infrastructure is real and scalable. As AI workloads continue to explode and centralized providers struggle with supply constraints, decentralized networks gain structural advantages in both economics and availability.

**The question isn't whether DePIN can compete, it's how fast centralized incumbents lose market share.**
